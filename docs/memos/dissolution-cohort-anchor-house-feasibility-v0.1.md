## 1. Purpose and gating role

Guardian's RT-2026-004 Q4 ruling installed an **anchor-house clause** as binding for TC-08's R8-closure-precondition standing: at least one large house in the Dissolution cohort must deliver **n ≥ 15 reform episodes** under TC-07's strict inclusion test. Without an anchor that exceeds the n < 15 weak-concordance ceiling, RT-2026-002 Q4 threshold (iii) (cross-form coverage without weak-concordance modal regime) cannot be satisfied by TC-08 alone — the entire R8 closure pathway then requires re-design, not just TC-08 v0.2 §4.2.

The TC-08 v0.2 authorisation (per RT-2026-004 §3) requires this feasibility check **before** cohort-selection commitment in §4.2. This note is that feasibility check.

The note does *not* perform extraction. It surveys the documentary record at desk-research resolution to identify whether any candidate house plausibly achieves n≥15 *under strict TC-07*. A formal extraction pass remains required before any final commitment.

## 2. The TC-07 strict-inclusion bar (recap)

Per `docs/protocols/human-timescales-reform-event-subtyping-protocol-v0.1.md` §3, an event is admitted as a reform-event if and only if all three conditions hold:

1. **Documentary ratification** — canon, decree, charter, ratified statute. Drafts and unratified working documents do not satisfy.
2. **Structural change in decision rights or membership rules** — change in who deliberates, votes, ratifies, overrules, authorises, or binds; or change in who counts as a member, conditions of admission/exclusion, obligations attached to membership.
3. **Multi-domain-legitimacy applicability** — for monastic houses, automatic where the house is recognised in canon law and produces ecclesially-binding artefacts beyond purely internal house rules.

Excluded explicitly (TC-07 §4): re-affirmation without operative change; administrative adjustment; rebranding; personnel succession under unchanged rules; pastoral instruction without canonical force; liturgical revision without disciplinary effect.

The exclusion list is consequential for monastic houses. Most documented "events" in monastic-house records — abbot elections, property grants, building campaigns, internal ceremonial revisions — fail the strict test. The reform-event population of any single house under strict TC-07 is therefore much smaller than its raw event chronology.

## 3. Candidate-house assessment

Four candidate houses surveyed, in order of plausibility as the n≥15 anchor.

### 3.1 Canterbury Christ Church (Cathedral Priory, c. 597 → 1540) — strongest anchor candidate

Canterbury Christ Church combines two attributes that thicken its reform-event pipeline beyond a typical Benedictine house: (a) cathedral-priory status produces dense canonical legislation and papal intervention; (b) the see of Canterbury's primatial role generates structurally-binding artefacts that fall within reach of the multi-domain-legitimacy criterion.

Likely TC-07-passing events (representative, not exhaustive):

- c. 997 — Formal monastic establishment under Dunstan-era reforms (structural).
- c. 1070–1089 — Lanfranc's *Monastic Constitutions* and creation of new pastoral and administrative structure (structural; major).
- 1093–1109 — Anselm-era grants of independence to priors; oblations and offerings settled on the priory (structural).
- 1162–1170 — Becket conflict and structural aftermath (structural; multi-domain).
- 1174 — Pope Alexander III privilege: the priory may elect its own prior, subject to conditions (structural; multi-domain via papal ratification).
- 1187 — Urban III confirmation; rules on prior-deposition added (structural).
- Various 13th-century papal visitations and confirmations.
- 14th- and 15th-century reform-and-visitation episodes documented in cathedral muniments.
- Pre-Dissolution episodes (e.g., 1530s royal visitations under Cromwell).

Conservative count under strict TC-07: **12–18**. Plausibly clears n≥15. Recommended as **primary anchor candidate**.

### 3.2 Glastonbury Abbey (founded c. 7th century → 1539) — Guardian's primary; uncertain at strict bar

Glastonbury has the longest lifespan of the candidates and the most architectural/abbatial-succession material in surviving documentation, but much of that material does *not* clear strict TC-07.

Likely TC-07-passing events:

- c. 940 — Dunstan installed; Benedictine Rule introduced (structural; major).
- 1077 — Egelnoth deposed by William the Conqueror; Thurstan installed (structural; jurisdictional).
- Various 12th-century papal grants and royal confirmations.
- Late-medieval reform-and-visitation episodes.
- 1539 — Dissolution (terminus; structural).

Architectural events (1184 fire and reconstruction; ~1342 Abbot Monington's remodelling) **do not** pass TC-07 §3.2 — they are physical, not structural in the decision-rights/membership-rules sense. Many abbatial successions occur under unchanged rules and are excluded under TC-07 §4.

Conservative count under strict TC-07: **8–12**. **May not anchor n≥15.** Glastonbury's documentary depth is genuine but heavily weighted toward physical and ceremonial rather than structural-canonical artefacts.

### 3.3 Westminster Abbey (c. 960 → 1540) — backup candidate

Royal-patronage history thickens the canonical-legislation pipeline. Major episodes: Edward the Confessor's foundation (1042–1065); Norman reforms; Plantagenet-era developments; multiple papal interventions; pre-Dissolution episodes.

Conservative count under strict TC-07: **10–15**. Borderline; may anchor depending on extraction-pass results.

### 3.4 Bury St Edmunds Abbey (c. 1020 → 1539) — backup candidate

Jocelin of Brakelond's chronicle (1173–1202) provides exceptionally granular coverage of Abbot Samson's governance reforms (asset-listing innovations, opposition management, royal-court access). Pre- and post-Samson periods are documented but less granularly.

Conservative count under strict TC-07: **8–12**. Less likely to anchor. Where Bury contributes most is *event-quality* (Samson-era events are extremely well-documented at the structural level) rather than event-count.

## 4. Verdict

**Anchor-house feasibility: PROVISIONALLY CONFIRMED on the Canterbury Christ Church candidacy, with extraction-pass validation required before TC-08 v0.2 §4.2 commitment.**

- **Canterbury Christ Church** plausibly delivers n ≥ 15 under strict TC-07 (conservative count 12–18). Cathedral-priory status and primatial-see role produce dense canonical legislation that monastic-only houses lack.
- **Glastonbury** does not robustly anchor n ≥ 15 under strict TC-07 (conservative count 8–12). Guardian's nomination of Glastonbury as primary candidate is based on architectural and abbatial-succession depth, much of which does not clear TC-07's strict structural-change bar.
- **Westminster and Bury St Edmunds** are backup candidates; both are borderline at n ≈ 10–15.

**The Guardian Q4 anchor-house ruling is satisfiable, but the named primary candidate (Glastonbury) is probably not the right anchor.** Canterbury Christ Church is the stronger candidate on documentary-supply grounds.

The verdict is *provisional* because desk-research cannot definitively count reform-events under TC-07 strict inclusion. A formal extraction pass against the four candidate houses is required before TC-08 v0.2 §4.2 commits to the cohort selection. Without the extraction pass, the n ≥ 15 claim for Canterbury rests on conservative qualitative assessment, not enumerated count.

## 5. Recommendation

For TC-08 v0.2 §4.2 commitment:

1. **Re-designate Canterbury Christ Church as primary anchor candidate**, with Glastonbury moved to backup. The Guardian Q4 ruling named Glastonbury as primary on the strength of memo §4.2's narrative; this feasibility note revises that selection on stricter inclusion-test grounds. The revision is logged here and propagated to TC-08 v0.2 with reasoning visible to Guardian for re-authorisation if required.
2. **Run a formal extraction pass on Canterbury Christ Church** against TC-07 strict inclusion before §4.2 commitment. Target: enumerate reform-events at year-or-decade precision for the c. 997 → 1540 window. If the count clears n ≥ 15, anchor is confirmed; if not, fall back to (3).
3. **If Canterbury fails extraction**, run sequential extraction on Westminster, then Bury St Edmunds, then Glastonbury. If none clear n ≥ 15 under strict TC-07, the anchor-house clause cannot be satisfied within the English-Dissolution candidate-set, and TC-08's R8-closure-precondition standing requires re-design (per the consequence ladder in RT-2026-004 §1).
4. **Cohort composition under Q4 stratified design (4 large / 4 medium / 4 small).** The "large" stratum receives whichever candidate clears the extraction pass as anchor; remaining "large"-stratum slots filled with the next-strongest candidates regardless of their individual n. Medium and small strata fill from standard-tier Benedictine and Cistercian houses with extant cartularies (e.g., Reading, Peterborough, Tewkesbury, Durham at large/medium boundary; smaller priories with documentary survival).
5. **TC-04 stratum-comparability coordination** (per RT-2026-004 §3 design-coherence requirement (2)) — the anchor's n ≥ 15 status is computed relative to TC-04's per-institution event counts; if TC-04 institutions yield n ≥ 15 under their own technical-event vs governance-event separation, threshold (iii) is satisfied across both deliverables; if not, the anchor's role becomes more load-bearing.

## 6. Open questions

- **Q-AH1.** Whether Canterbury Christ Church's primatial-see role makes it *too* multi-domain to function as a clean Dissolution-cohort anchor. The TC-07 §3.3 multi-domain-legitimacy criterion is satisfied trivially by any cathedral priory, but the Becket conflict (1162–1170) and other Canterbury-specific episodes blur the boundary between "the cathedral priory's reform-event" and "the see of Canterbury's reform-event." The eventual extraction pass must declare the boundary explicitly.
- **Q-AH2.** Whether the Dunstan-era reforms (c. 940–997) should be counted once or distributed across Canterbury and Glastonbury (Dunstan was abbot at Glastonbury before Canterbury). Drafter recommendation: distribute, with explicit declaration that the Dunstan reforms are programme-level not house-level and may appear in multiple house chronologies under distinct event IDs.
- **Q-AH3.** Whether to run the extraction pass under Architect direction or hand off to a medievalist domain consultant. Drafter recommendation: pilot-extraction by Author/Architect to validate the TC-07 inclusion-test application; full extraction by domain consultant once the pilot establishes the inclusion-test interpretation conventions.
- **Q-AH4.** Whether Guardian re-authorisation is required for the Glastonbury → Canterbury anchor swap. The Q4 ruling named Glastonbury but did not lock the choice; the anchor-clause logic is satisfied by *any* large house with n ≥ 15, not specifically by Glastonbury. Drafter recommendation: notify Guardian of the swap, but do not block on re-authorisation since the structural ruling is preserved.
