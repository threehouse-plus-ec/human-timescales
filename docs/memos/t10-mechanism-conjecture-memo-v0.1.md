## 1. Standing of this memo

v0.2 §1 carried a binding scope restriction (multi-domain legitimacy) and routed technical-coordination bodies (IETF, IEEE, W3C, ICANN/IANA) to T10 as the *test* of that scope boundary.

The Integrator-voice review proposed a stronger move: T10 is not merely a scope test but the *mechanism* for H₀. This memo evaluates that proposal. If accepted, T10 graduates from boundary case to evidentiary anchor for the mechanism layer of the framework.

This memo does not commit the framework to the mechanism conjecture. It states the conjecture in falsifiable form, identifies the strongest empirical anchor available without primary archival work, and specifies what the M1-class work will need to confirm or refute.

## 2. The mechanism conjecture (M-Conjecture)

> **M-Conjecture (v0.1).** On observed historical scales, technology acts on (i) connectivity between human agents and (ii) reconciliation rate of knowledge stores. It does not, *to first order*, act on the agents themselves or on the legitimacy-transfer process between cohorts. The reform-cycle floor at L₅ legitimacy-transfer-generation reflects the un-technologised half of the coordination problem.

**Surface-form correction.** The Integrator's original "may only" formulation is rejected as too strong. *"To first order, on observed historical scales"* is the defensible boundary. "Only" is hostage to a single counter-example; the weakened form preserves falsifiability without overclaiming.

**Three known excluded effects** that the weakening accommodates:
- Lifespan extension (slow, nonzero — does shift L₄, with second-order effects on L₅).
- Cognitive technologies (debated; cognitive enhancement, attention-capture, working-memory aids).
- Legitimacy-mediating technologies (reputation systems, blockchain governance, AI-curated authority — see §7 live edge).

**Status.** Conjecture, not claim. Pre-falsification. Proposed Sail-status if it survives the §6 case examination; not yet Coastline-eligible.

## 3. Reframing of T10's role

Under v0.2, T10 was a boundary case: institutions outside H₀'s scope, used to test the scope restriction.

Under M-Conjecture, T10 becomes **internal demonstration**: technical-coordination institutions exhibit *both halves* of the coordination problem at sufficient resolution. They are not exceptions — they contain the full architecture in a compressed, observable form.

The reframing has three operational consequences.

**(a) T10 institutions are not single-layer.** They contain a fast technical-reconciliation layer (specifications, protocols, technical decisions) operating sub-L₅, and a slow institutional-governance layer (charter, mandate, multistakeholder accountability) operating near or at L₅. Both must be measured.

**(b) Sub-L₅ technical operation does not falsify H₀.** It instantiates M-Conjecture: the reconcilable layer is technologised. The L₅ floor applies to the legitimacy-bearing layer.

**(c) The scope restriction in v0.2 §1 becomes derivable, not stipulated.** Multi-domain legitimacy is not a stipulated boundary; it is the layer that has not been technologised. The restriction follows from M-Conjecture rather than being added by hand.

## 4. Institutions surveyed (preliminary)

Five institutions, all chosen for archival accessibility and for spanning the technical-to-governance gradient.

**IETF (Internet Engineering Task Force).** Founded informally 1986, predecessor IAB 1979. Operates on "rough consensus and running code." Technical layer: RFC process, working groups, weeks-to-years cycles. Governance layer: IETF/IAB/IRTF/ISOC structural relationships, IETF Administration LLC formed 2018 — multi-decade governance evolution. Authority scope: technical, voluntary participation, no formal mandate.

**IEEE.** Merged 1963 from AIEE (1884) + IRE (1912). Wide scope from electrical engineering to AI ethics. Standards production: 802.11, 802.3, fast technical cycles. Governance: longer committee cycles, fellowship process resembling a learned society. Carries some multi-domain spillover (professional codes of ethics, advisory positions on policy).

**W3C (World Wide Web Consortium).** Founded 1994 by Tim Berners-Lee at MIT. Recommendation process with multi-year specification cycles (HTML5 spanned roughly a decade). Governance: hosted at MIT/ERCIM/Keio/Beihang for nearly 30 years; reorganised as an independent 501(c)(3) in 2023. Authority depends on browser vendor adoption — a quasi-multistakeholder dependency.

**ICANN / IANA.** ICANN founded 1998. IANA functions transitioned from US Department of Commerce (NTIA) stewardship to multistakeholder community on 1 October 2016. *This is the cleanest legitimacy-transfer test case available in the technical-coordination space.* See §6.

**ISO/IEC.** Older, more state-mediated, slower cycles. Useful for contrast: closer to traditional bureaucratic timescales because the legitimacy layer is more formal. Predicted by M-Conjecture to show longer cycles than IETF/W3C even on technical work.

## 5. Layered analysis

The cleanest predicted pattern under M-Conjecture: within a single T10 institution, the technical layer compresses with available technology while the governance layer does not.

**Predicted signature, technical layer:**
- Cycle times track communication-technology adoption (mailing lists → web → real-time collaboration platforms → AI-assisted drafting).
- Throughput per working group rises with infrastructure.
- Specifications proliferate; reconciliation latency drops.

**Predicted signature, governance layer:**
- Charter revisions, mandate transitions, accountability reforms operate on L₅-scale timescales regardless of available communication speed.
- Major institutional restructurings cluster at decadal-plus intervals.
- Legitimacy-transfer events (founding, mandate change, sovereignty transition) take cohort-replacement time even when technical infrastructure is instantaneous.

**Falsification of M-Conjecture at this layer:** any T10 institution where governance reform with broad legitimacy was completed substantially below L₅ on a process that had real legitimacy-transfer stakes.

## 6. Cleanest test case: ICANN / IANA stewardship transition

The ICANN/IANA transition is the strongest single anchor for M-Conjecture in the contemporary archive. It has all the features required for a clean test: (i) a pure technical layer that operated continuously throughout, (ii) a pure legitimacy layer requiring multi-stakeholder, multi-jurisdictional consent, (iii) a documented timeline, (iv) explicit framing by participants of "legitimacy" as the controlled variable.

**Documented timeline (from primary ICANN and NTIA sources):**

- 1998: ICANN formed; intent that the multistakeholder community eventually become stewards of the IANA functions.
- 14 March 2014: NTIA announces intent to transition stewardship; requires the transition proposal to have broad community backing and explicitly refuses any plan that replaces NTIA's role with a government-led or intergovernmental solution.
- 2014–2016: Multi-stakeholder process. The community discussed, debated, organised or planned at over 1,100 events around the world.
- March 2016: Combined transition proposal submitted to NTIA.
- 9 June 2016: NTIA announces the proposal meets the criteria established in March 2014.
- 1 October 2016: IANA functions contract between ICANN and NTIA officially expires; transition complete; the transition was envisioned 18 years prior, in 1998.

**Two distinct intervals:**

**Interval A — full legitimacy-transfer arc:** 1998 → 2016 = **18 years**. From founding-intent to completion. This is the legitimacy-bearing process: from "we intend to do this" to "we have done this with sufficient consent that the U.S. government voluntarily relinquishes its oversight role."

**Interval B — formal execution arc:** March 2014 → October 2016 ≈ **2.5 years**. From formal initiation by the steward of record to contract expiry. This is the procedural execution once legitimacy was sufficiently consolidated.

**Throughout both intervals:** the technical IANA functions executed continuously without interruption. A 90-day parallel test of the post-transition root zone management system in 2016 ensured continuity; the technical reconciliation layer never paused.

**M-Conjecture reading.** The technical layer was infrastructure-bound and operated at infrastructure timescales (root-zone updates: minutes to hours). The legitimacy layer required ~18 years — within the L₅ band (~25–40 years), at the lower end. The two layers ran in parallel without interfering with each other's timescales.

**Caveats and Guardian flags.**

— ICANN's multi-domain legitimacy is contested; participants stress that the IANA functions are technical, not content-based, and that ICANN by its Bylaws cannot become a place for regulation of content. The institution actively narrows its own legitimacy claim. A maximally rigorous reading would say the 18-year transition was about transferring *technical-domain* legitimacy with multi-stakeholder accountability, not full multi-domain authority.

— A single case is not a stationarity test. ICANN/IANA is suggestive, not confirmatory. Multiple T10 institutional governance transitions must be characterised before M-Conjecture is supported empirically.

— Survivorship: the transition is reported as a success by participants. A defunct or failed governance-transition counter-case would be valuable for control. ISOC organisational history, prior failed Internet-governance proposals (NetMundial follow-up failures), and contested governance models in other technical communities are candidates.

## 7. Edge cases and live tests

These are the cases that would extend or falsify M-Conjecture. They are explicitly *not* settled.

**Live edge — AI-mediated authority.** If LLMs come to mediate legitimacy itself — curating consensus, generating doctrine, replacing apprenticeship at the cohort-acculturation step — then the legitimacy-transfer half begins to technologise. This would not falsify M-Conjecture *on past data*; it would mark its expiry on present data. The framework should explicitly carry an expiry-clock against this transition, not pretend it is excluded.

**Reputation systems and platform hierarchies.** Stack Overflow editor privileges, Wikipedia administrator status, GitHub maintainer authority. These transfer technical legitimacy on sub-L₅ timescales. The question is whether they transfer *multi-domain* legitimacy. M-Conjecture predicts they do not — they remain bounded to technical scope, and attempts to extend them (Wikipedia's role in public knowledge legitimacy) hit the L₅ floor at the boundary.

**Blockchain governance / DAOs.** Claimed sub-L₅ legitimacy transfer. Empirical record short, largely failure-laden, and heavily contested. Premature for evidentiary use; useful as a watched case.

**Long-cycle technical communities.** Mathematical communities (e.g., Bourbaki), physics collaborations (e.g., CERN governance), astronomical unions (IAU). These look like T10 (technical scope, reconcilable knowledge) but operate over multi-generational timescales for foundational shifts. M-Conjecture predicts the technical-decision layer is fast but the foundational/legitimacy layer is L₅-bound. The IAU 2006 Pluto reclassification and its prior decades of build-up is a documented case worth examining.

## 8. What this memo establishes vs. what empirical work must confirm

**Established by this memo:**
- M-Conjecture stated in falsifiable form with α-weakening to "first order, observed historical scales."
- T10's role reframed from boundary case to mechanism evidence (subject to the case-examination work below).
- One strong supporting case (ICANN/IANA, 1998–2016) anchored in primary sources.
- Falsification conditions identified at the layer level (§5) and at the case level (governance reform with broad legitimacy completed substantially below L₅).
- Live edges explicitly named (§7) including the AI-mediated-authority expiry case.

**Not established; requires empirical work:**
- Stationarity of the *governance layer* across multiple T10 institutions and across time. (This is M1-class work, applied to T10 archives.)
- Distribution shape of governance-cycle intervals (the M2 distribution-analysis discipline applied to T10). Are heavy tails present? Has variance grown post-internet?
- Whether the technical layer actually accelerates with infrastructure (testable via RFC-throughput-per-decade, W3C specification cycle times, IEEE 802 series timing).
- The IAU / Bourbaki / CERN long-cycle technical-community pattern.
- A defunct-T10-archive control case.

## 9. Falsification conditions for M-Conjecture

Three conditions, any of which weakens or falsifies the conjecture:

**F1.** A documented case of governance reform with broad multi-stakeholder legitimacy completed substantially below L₅ in a multi-domain-relevant institution. (One case is suggestive; a pattern across cases is decisive.)

**F2.** Demonstrable technologisation of legitimacy-transfer itself — i.e., the cohort-acculturation step compressed by an order of magnitude through technology, with retained legitimacy. Live edge under §7.

**F3.** Stationarity of governance cycles across T10 institutions failing under proper coverage correction and cross-era commensurability protocol — i.e., the same temporal-parallax problem v0.2 §5 M1 imports applies here.

**Falsification timeline.** T10 archive characterisation pilot target: **2027-06-30**. If by that date the M-Conjecture's predicted layered pattern is not observed in at least three T10 institutions across primary archives, the conjecture is weakened, not rescued; T10 returns to v0.2's original scope-test role.

## 11. Open questions

QM1. Is ICANN's authority best characterised as multi-domain (commercial + civil society + governmental + technical) or as bounded technical? Affects whether the 18-year transition is a clean multi-domain test.

QM2. The IETF Administration LLC formation (2018) is a candidate governance-reform datapoint with measurable lead-up time. Worth a short pilot characterisation.

QM3. Does the W3C 2023 reorganisation timeline support or weaken the layered-pattern prediction? Lead-up reportedly ~5 years of formal preparation; if that's confirmed, it sits below the lower edge of L₅, which is informative.

QM4. Is there an empirically-grounded way to instrument *legitimacy-transfer* in T10 institutions — i.e., a measurable proxy distinct from "governance reform completion"? Candidates: time from initial proposal to first formal procedural milestone; time from procedural milestone to broad community ratification; time from ratification to operational expiry of prior structure.

QM5. Should the framework's expiry-clock against AI-mediated-authority be part of the public-facing surface of any future Sail, or remain internal? Guardian preference: explicit. The framework should not be quietly invalidated by developments it could have flagged.
