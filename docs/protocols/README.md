# Protocols

Operational methodological protocols. Each protocol fixes the rules under which downstream empirical claims may be issued; protocols carry no empirical claim of their own.

## Active protocols

- `human-timescales-commensurability-protocol-v0.1.md` — TC-06 deliverable. Cross-era commensurability for M1 reform-cycle archaeology. Dual-measurement (calendar-time + institution-internal-time) selected. Gates TC-01.
- `human-timescales-reform-event-subtyping-protocol-v0.1.md` — TC-07 deliverable. Inclusion test, exclusion test, and three-sub-type taxonomy (doctrinal / structural / disciplinary) with multi-tagging required where evidence is positive. Inter-coder reliability target ≥80%. Gates TC-01.

## Discipline

Protocols are versioned (v0.1, v0.2, …). Each carries:

- Endorsement Marker (provisional, internal).
- Inheritance from the canonical scoping document's non-prescription clause.
- Stance log.
- Procedural-falsification statement: which downstream claims are invalidated by failure to invoke the protocol correctly.

## Distinction from memos and sails

- **Protocols** are binding methodological infrastructure. They do not argue; they specify.
- **Memos** investigate threads and conjectures. They argue.
- **Sails** are interpretive essays under the T4 kill-criterion.

A protocol that begins to argue should be split: the argument graduates to a memo, the operational rules remain in the protocol.
