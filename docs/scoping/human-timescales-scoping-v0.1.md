## 1. Working hypothesis (frozen for testing)

> **H₀ (Fixed-Hardware Hypothesis):** Human collective systems exhibit characteristic minimum response and reform timescales that are bounded below by quasi-invariant biological and cognitive constraints (lifespan, generational replacement, individual decision latency, social-network propagation rate). These timescales have *not* substantially shortened across recorded history despite repeated changes in communication and computation infrastructure.

> **H₁ (Pressure Corollary):** Current technological developments (real-time global communication, algorithmic decision support, AI-mediated coordination) impose demands on collective systems that exceed the H₀ floor. The resulting gap manifests as instability, oscillation, or breakdown of error-correction layers — not as commensurate acceleration of the systems themselves.

**Falsification handle.** H₀ predicts that empirical reform-cycle distributions across centuries are stationary (after correcting for measurement coverage). H₁ predicts increased variance and increased frequency of architectural reversal in recent decades, *not* a uniform speed-up. A simple uniform speed-up across eras would falsify both.

⚠ **Guardian flag.** "Hardware" is a load-bearing word here and a known site of category error. The hypothesis must be insulated from technological determinism *and* from biologisation of politics. See §9.

## 2. Thread inventory

**T1 — Bayer / Humanocracy / managerial decoupling.**

Existing dossier on trans-generational creative traditions. Scout Flag SF-7 (custodial attenuation) was stress-tested across multiple traditions and indicates that custodial transmission has a characteristic decay envelope on roughly a 2–3 generation scale, independent of medium. This is a candidate empirical anchor for H₀.

**T3 — Long-lived institutions.**
Catholic Church, ancient universities (Bologna, Oxford), Royal Society, monastic orders, common-law courts, central banks. These institutions have explicit archives of reform cycles measured against their own continuity. They are the natural laboratories for H₀.

**T4 — ADM-EC clock framework.**
The causal-geometry view of clocks (L_source / L_apparatus / L_comparison; η(τ) = L_comparison / cτ) is a candidate analogy *only*. The risk is loose metaphor; the opportunity is that "causal efficiency" of a deliberation cycle may have a precise social analogue. This must be staged as Sail, not Coastline.

**T5 — Council / deliberation timescales.**
Council-3 / Council-5 / Council-7 deliberation cycles, Chart-Transition Protocol, holonomy detection. Internal data on how long actual deliberation takes when conducted with explicit architecture. Useful as a microscale calibration point.

**T6 — Familien-Charta and household systems.**
The smallest collective system available. Decision-cycle data at this scale exists in the form of family operations records. Useful as the lower-bound calibration for "minimum coordination cost."

**T7 — Technological pressure indicators.**
News-cycle compression, regulatory response lag (e.g., AI governance delay relative to deployment), reaction times in financial markets vs. legislative bodies, episode duration of public attention. All candidate proxies for "pressure" in H₁.

**T8 — Lifespan and generational replacement.**
Demographic data: average tenure of CEOs, mean age of legislators, half-life of expert cohorts in scientific fields, mean career length. Direct candidate evidence for the biological floor.

**T9 — Communication-bandwidth history.**
Speech rate, reading rate, working-memory capacity. These are the hardest-floor candidates — they have not changed across recorded history. Cross-check against any claimed acceleration.

## 3. What "fixed hardware timescale" actually means

The Guardian flags that this phrase smuggles five distinct claims. They must be separated before any empirical work.

| Layer | Candidate timescale | Fixed by |
|---|---|---|
| L₁ Cognitive | seconds–minutes | individual decision latency, working-memory window |
| L₂ Daily | hours | sleep cycle, attention budget |
| L₃ Career | decades | individual learning curve, expertise ramp |
| L₄ Lifespan | ~80 yr | biological mortality |
| L₅ Generational | ~25–30 yr | reproductive interval, custodial transmission |

Different collective phenomena live at different layers. A reform of a deliberative body operates near L₃–L₅. A trading-floor protocol operates at L₁. The hypothesis must specify *which* layer it claims is rate-limiting for *which* class of system. A blanket "hardware floor" is not testable.

**Provisional refinement.** The strongest version of H₀ to test first is restricted to L₅: *generational replacement is the rate-limiting timescale for institutional reform with persistent legitimacy.*

## 4. What acceleration would look like vs. would not

If H₀ is **wrong**, we expect to see:

- Reform cycles in long-lived institutions shortening monotonically over centuries.
- Mean tenure of leadership shortening proportionally to communication speed.
- Custodial-transmission decay envelope (T2/SF-7) compressing.
- Emergence of stable institutional forms with characteristic timescales below L₅.

If H₀ is **right** but H₁ is **right**, we expect to see:

- Reform-cycle distributions stationary in *centre*, but with growing *variance* and growing frequency of *reversed* reforms (oscillation, not progression).
- Increasing gap between external pressure rate (T7) and internal response rate.
- Increased rate of "fashionable" architectural changes that do not propagate to L₅ depth (e.g. Humanocracy-style flattenings that revert within a single tenure).
- Symptom inventory: silent error propagation, breakwater erosion, restoration of hidden hierarchy under flat labels (the Zappos/Spotify pattern).

The Bayer case becomes a *predicted instance*, not a precedent — falsifiable on a 5–15 year window.

## 5. Methodological candidates

**M1 — Reform-cycle archaeology.**
Compile dated reform events for a fixed institutional set (e.g., Catholic Church councils; English common-law statutory consolidations; Royal Society charters; central-bank mandates). Measure inter-event intervals. Test for stationarity vs. monotonic compression.

**M2 — Tenure series.**
Compile leader tenure (CEOs, heads of state, university rectors, religious heads) over the longest available windows. Test for trend vs. stationarity.

**M3 — Custodial-attenuation re-analysis.**
Apply SF-7 instrumentation from GLHC to additional traditions; test whether the decay envelope is era-invariant.

**M4 — Pressure-vs-response gap.**
For a defined recent period, measure external-event rate (T7) and internal-response rate (legislative cycle, regulatory adoption, institutional reform completion). The *ratio* over time is the H₁ signal.

**M5 — Ledger entry.**
Frame the whole programme as a Breakwater claim ledger: H₀ and H₁ as a two-claim entry, with predictions, constraints, and discriminant condition explicit. Default verdict at scoping time: UNDERDETERMINED.

## 9. Guardian risk register

This is the section that protects the programme from its own most attractive failure modes.

**R1 — Biologisation of politics.**
"Hardware-determined timescales" is rhetorically adjacent to biological determinism, technocratic justification of slow institutions, and exclusionary "natural-pace" arguments. The framing must be: *minimum* timescales, not *correct* timescales. A floor is not a target.

**R2 — Technological determinism in the inverse direction.**
The H₁ "pressure" framing must not silently assume that technology *should* set the pace and human systems are *failing* to keep up. The question is symmetric: is the pressure adaptive or pathological?

**R3 — Survivorship bias in long-lived institutions.**
The institutions available in the archive are precisely those that survived. Their reform cycles are not a fair sample of all attempted reforms.

**R4 — "Hardware" as motte-and-bailey.**
Slipping between L₁ (cognitive latency) and L₅ (generational replacement) is the chief opacity risk. Each empirical claim must specify its layer.

**R5 — Harbour vocabulary inflation.**
Importing ADM-EC, η_opt, etc. into the social domain risks weakening their meaning in the physical domain. Use Sail status; do not let metaphor backflow into Coastline.

**R6 — Reform fashion as data.**
"Humanocracy" and similar movements are themselves subject to fashion cycles. Distinguishing genuine architectural change from rebranding is a measurement problem, not a definition problem.

**R7 — Ethical risk of policy implication.**
Any output that could be read as "institutions cannot move faster, therefore demands for faster reform are illegitimate" is a Guardian-veto-eligible move. Conversely, "institutions must be sped up to match technology" is also veto-eligible. The programme is descriptive; prescriptive uses require additional argument and must be flagged.

## 10. Open questions

Q1. Which of L₁–L₅ is the actual rate-limiter for *institutional reform with legitimacy*? Hypothesis: L₅. Test via M1.

Q2. Is the SF-7 custodial-attenuation envelope era-invariant? Test via M3.

Q3. Does the pressure-response gap (M4) widen monotonically over the past century, or step at specific technological transitions (telegraph, radio, internet, LLMs)?

Q4. Are there counter-examples — institutions that *have* sustainably accelerated their reform cycle without losing legitimacy? Candidate: technical standards bodies (IETF, IEEE). These may show that *narrow-scope* coordination *can* operate below L₅.

Q5. Does the Harbour itself have a predicted minimum reform timescale, given its declared architecture? This is a self-application question and a Council-eligible deliberation.

Q6. What is the relationship between this hypothesis and the existing Topological Thesis (CL-2026-002)? Both invoke architectural invariants; the connection — if any — needs articulation, not assumption.
