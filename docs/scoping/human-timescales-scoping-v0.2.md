## 0a. Non-prescription clause (permanent, extraction-safe)

This document describes minimum coordination timescales as observed phenomena. It does not endorse them as correct, optimal, or politically legitimate. **A floor is not a target.** Any extracted use of this material to prescribe institutional pace — whether arguing that institutions cannot or should not move faster, or that they must — is unauthorised by the document's framing. The work is descriptive; prescriptive uses require independent argument and explicit additional warrant.

## 1. Working hypothesis (frozen for testing)

> **H₀ (Legitimacy-Transfer-Generation Hypothesis):** Institutional reform with persistent multi-domain legitimacy is bounded below by a *legitimacy-transfer generation* — the minimum interval over which an institution's decision-making cohort can be fully replaced and its new members acculturated to recognised authority. This interval is typically L₅-scale (~25–40 years), measurable through tenure-plus-initiation-plus-apprenticeship windows, and has not substantially shortened across recorded history despite changes in communication and computation infrastructure.

> **H₁ (Pressure Corollary):** Current technological developments (real-time global communication, algorithmic decision support, AI-mediated coordination) impose external demand rates that exceed the H₀ floor. The signature is *not* uniform speed-up of institutions, but **second-order properties**: increased variance in reform-cycle distributions, increased reversal frequency, oscillation between architectural fashions, and silent error propagation where explicit error-correction layers have been dismantled.

**Scope restriction (binding).** H₀ applies to *multi-domain legitimacy institutions* — those whose authority crosses spheres (legal, moral, economic, epistemic). Single-domain technical-coordination bodies (e.g., IETF, IEEE) are explicitly outside H₀'s scope and are routed to T10 as the test of the scope boundary itself.

**Falsification handle.** H₀ predicts that reform-cycle distributions across centuries are stationary in centre after coverage correction. H₁ predicts increased variance and increased reversal frequency in recent decades, *not* uniform compression. Uniform speed-up across eras falsifies both. Coverage-corrected stationarity that disappears under cross-era commensurability protocol (see §5 M1) is *not* a confirmation but an artefact.

⚠ **Guardian flag retained.** The "hardware" working metaphor is preserved internally for shorthand only. Public-facing or extracted text uses *embodied coordination limits* or *biological-cognitive lower bounds*. The non-prescription clause (§0a) governs any external surface.

## 3. Layer architecture and property dimensions

The L₁–L₅ decomposition separates five candidate timescales. Architect review (2026-04-27) added two essential refinements: (a) layer interdependence, (b) property-type distinction.

| Layer | Candidate timescale | Bounded by | Property type |
|---|---|---|---|
| L₁ Cognitive | seconds–minutes | individual decision latency, working-memory window | latency |
| L₂ Daily | hours | sleep cycle, attention budget | latency |
| L₃ Career | decades | individual learning curve, expertise ramp | throughput |
| L₄ Lifespan | ~80 yr | biological mortality | throughput |
| **L₅ Legitimacy-transfer generation** | **~25–40 yr** | **cohort replacement + acculturation to recognised authority** | **throughput** |

**L₅ definition (binding for v0.2):** *the minimum interval over which an institution's decision-making cohort can be fully replaced and its new members acculturated to the point of recognised authority.* This is **not** strict biological reproduction. It can decouple from biological generation through compressed apprenticeship, externalised institutional memory, or living-tradition shortcuts. It is directly measurable via tenure + initiation + apprenticeship windows.

**Property-type note (E2).** L₁/L₂ are latencies; L₃/L₄/L₅ are throughputs. H₁ predictions are about a third type — **jitter / variance / oscillation** — second-order properties of the throughput distributions, not first-order speed claims. The H₁ signature is variance growth and reversal frequency, not mean compression. Reading H₁ as "things are speeding up" is a category error.

**Layer interdependence (Architect §3 a).** L₅ is fed by L₃–L₄. Effective L₅ can shift without biological change if custodial function transfers through compressed channels (e.g., externalised memory, formal apprenticeship compression). This is part of what the empirical work tests, not an assumption it imports.

**Provisional refinement.** The strongest version of H₀ to test first is restricted to L₅, with multi-domain legitimacy scope, and is tested via stationarity-plus-variance signatures, not via mean compression alone.

## 4. What acceleration would look like vs. would not

If H₀ is **wrong**, expected signatures:
- Reform cycles in long-lived multi-domain-legitimacy institutions shorten monotonically over centuries (after coverage correction and cross-era commensurability adjustment).
- Mean tenure of leadership shortens proportionally to communication speed.
- Custodial-transmission decay envelope (T2 / SF-7) compresses.
- Stable institutional forms emerge with characteristic timescales below L₅ while retaining multi-domain legitimacy.

If H₀ is **right** but H₁ is **right**, expected signatures:
- Reform-cycle distributions stationary in centre, but with growing variance and growing kurtosis (heavy tails).
- Increasing pressure-vs-completion gap (de facto, not de jure).
- Increased rate of fashionable architectural changes that do not propagate to L₅ depth.
- Symptom inventory: silent error propagation, breakwater erosion, restoration of hidden hierarchy under flat labels.
- T1 (Bayer) becomes a predicted instance on its 5–15 year window.

## 5. Methodological architecture

Five methods. Each carries explicit constraints from Architect and Scout review.

### M1 — Reform-cycle archaeology

**Pilot site:** Catholic ecumenical councils (Nicaea 325 → Vatican II 1962–65). 21 dated events, 16-century window.

**Null models (binding, Architect M1):**
(a) constant Poisson rate (memoryless reform);
(b) clustered events around external shocks.
Discriminant: are inter-event intervals stable after controlling for external-stimulus frequency?

**Cross-era commensurability protocol (binding, E3, promoted from R8).** Reform-cycle duration is measured in calendar time, but institutions construct time differently across eras (liturgical, fiscal, administrative). Before any stationarity claim, the pilot must specify how cross-era duration measurements are commensurate. Three candidate protocols:
(i) calendar-time only, with explicit acknowledgement of construction-asymmetry;
(ii) institution-internal time (e.g., reform-events-per-prelate-tenure);
(iii) dual measurement, reporting both and any divergence.
**No stationarity claim is admissible without explicit protocol declaration.** Failure to declare invalidates the inference.

**Reform-event definition (binding, Scout signal 3, prerequisite).** *Reform = structural change in decision rights or membership rules, evidenced by documentary ratification.* Sub-typed into doctrinal / structural / disciplinary (Architect M1, second bullet). L₅ may hold for one sub-type and not others; sub-type must be reported, not aggregated.

**Survivorship-bias control (recommended, Scout signal 2).** Pilot includes feasibility check on at least one defunct institutional archive. Suggested: Knights Templar (dissolved 1312, substantial documentary trace) or a dissolved monastic order with extant cartulary.

### M2 — Tenure series

**Distribution analysis binding (Architect M2):** measure variance and kurtosis, not means alone. Mean tenure can stay stationary while distributions develop heavier tails (short tenures + entrenchment co-existing). That pattern is consistent with H₁ pressure.

**Sources:** CEOs, heads of state, university rectors, religious heads, central-bank governors, scientific-society presidents.

### M3 — Custodial-attenuation re-analysis

**Diversification binding (Architect M3):** at least one non-artisanal, non-monastic tradition added (suggested: a scientific school lineage; a legal-tradition lineage). Avoids the critique that SF-7 only holds for slow-intentional traditions.

**Cleanest test for H₀ at L₅** if successful — anchors the entire programme through mutual reinforcement with M1 (non-circular, since the underlying instrumentation differs).

### M4 — Pressure-vs-response gap

**Operationalisation binding (Architect M4):** measure *completed decision rate* (actual policy change, not deliberation start) vs. *external perturbation arrival rate*. De jure response rate (rule) and de facto response rate (completion) must be reported separately.

**Sail effort acknowledged.** Operationalising "completion" is itself a non-trivial Sail; pilot work goes here before scaling.

### M5 — Ledger entry

Two-claim Breakwater package: H₀ + H₁ with explicit discriminant condition.

**Falsification timeline (binding, E4):**
- M1 pilot (Catholic ecumenical councils) target completion: **2026-12-31**.
- If pilot does not yield a stationary distribution after coverage correction *and* declared cross-era commensurability protocol, H₀-L₅ is **weakened**, not rescued.
- No motte-and-bailey escape: scope cannot be re-narrowed post-hoc without the revision being logged as a substantive theoretical retreat.

## 9. Risk register (Guardian)

**R1 — Biologisation of politics.** "Hardware-determined timescales" is rhetorically adjacent to biological determinism and to exclusionary "natural-pace" arguments. Framing must remain *minimum* timescales, not *correct* ones. Governed by §0a non-prescription clause.

**R2 — Inverse technological determinism.** H₁ "pressure" must not silently assume technology *should* set pace and human systems *should* keep up. Symmetric question: is the pressure adaptive or pathological?

**R3 — Survivorship bias in long-lived institutions.** The archive consists precisely of institutions that survived. If failed institutions had systematically shorter reform cycles (oscillated to death), observed survivor-stationarity is a selection effect. Structural control via M1 defunct-archive feasibility check (Templars or comparable).

**R4 — "Hardware" as motte-and-bailey.** Slipping between L₁ and L₅ is the chief opacity risk. Each empirical claim specifies its layer. Falsification timeline (E4) blocks post-hoc layer-switching.

**R5 — Harbour vocabulary inflation.** Importing ADM-EC, η_opt into the social domain risks weakening physical-domain meaning. Sail-status and kill-criterion (§T4) prevent inflation.

**R6 — Reform fashion as data.** Branded movements ("Humanocracy") are themselves subject to fashion cycles. Distinguishing genuine architectural change from rebranding is a measurement problem within M1 reform-event definition.

**R7 — Ethical risk of policy implication.** Any output readable as "institutions cannot move faster, therefore demands for faster reform are illegitimate" is veto-eligible. Inverse equally veto-eligible. Governed by §0a; remains operative through every downstream deliverable.

**R8 — Temporal parallax (promoted to methodological constraint, §5 M1).** Reform-cycle duration may be an institution-specific construct, not a cross-era variable. Cross-era commensurability protocol mandatory.

**R9 — Sokal/Bricmont risk on T4.** Importing physics vocabulary into social-science discourse has documented misuse history. Kill-criterion on T4 (§6) is the structural answer.

Q1. Is L₅ — *as legitimacy-transfer generation* — the actual rate-limiter for multi-domain-legitimacy reform? Test via M1 + M3.

Q2. Is the SF-7 custodial-attenuation envelope era-invariant across diversified traditions? Test via M3.

Q3. Does the pressure-completion gap (M4 de facto) widen monotonically or step at specific technological transitions?

Q4 (resolved into T10 scope test). Technical coordination bodies (IETF, IEEE) operate below L₅. T10 tests whether they bear multi-domain legitimacy. If yes, the scope restriction fails.

Q5. Does the Harbour itself have a predicted minimum reform timescale? Self-application via internal latency-tracking protocol (§6), with Guardian non-feedback constraint.

Q6. Relationship to CL-2026-002 (Topological Thesis). Both invoke architectural invariants at different scales (deliberation topology vs. institutional history). v0.2 deferred mapping; v0.3 should articulate whether L₅ is a coastline-style invariant in the Topological-Thesis sense or a sail-status interpretation.

Q7. Layer interdependence: under what conditions can effective L₅ shift without biological change? Compressed apprenticeship, externalised memory, living-tradition shortcuts. Empirical question, not a theoretical one.
