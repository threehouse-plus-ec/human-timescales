## 1. Why these reviewers, why this card, why now

TC-07 is the second of the two gating prerequisites for TC-01, alongside TC-06. Where TC-06 disciplines the *time axis* of reform-cycle archaeology (commensurability), TC-07 disciplines the *event-coding axis* (what counts as a reform event, and how it is sub-typed). The two protocols couple at the verdict-issuance layer: a stationarity claim under TC-06 v0.2 §5 condition 4 (channel-concordance with three-tier power rule) is computed on TC-07-defined event sets stratified into sub-types.

**Guardian's primary jurisdiction (R3 + R4 discipline):**
- **R3 survivorship-bias.** Multi-tagging strictness (TC-07 §6 forbids predominance-based single-tagging) interacts with documentary-survival asymmetry: modern councils have richer records, producing higher multi-tag rates and era-correlated sub-type stratification bias (SF-9, lodged in horizon-signals 2026-04-28). Without era-stratified sampling for inter-coder reliability, the multi-tag inflation looks like temporal trend but is source heterogeneity.
- **R4 motte-and-bailey.** TC-07 §3 inclusion test and §4 exclusion test together produce a strict event population. Boundary cases — encyclicals/motu-proprio without canonical force, administrative-adjustment-vs-structural-change, rebranding-vs-renaming-with-power-change — are exactly where rescue-by-relabelling moves can enter under empirical pressure. The pre-declaration discipline that Guardian RT-2026-002 Q3 installed for TC-06's per-interval divergence-cause tags is the analogue: TC-07 needs an equivalent pre-declaration discipline for its inclusion/exclusion ruling.

**Domain consultant's co-jurisdiction (council historiography for TC-01-side questions):**
- Historiographic interpretation of inclusion-test edge cases (Q1, Q4 below) — where TC-07 §3 and §4 are operationally specified but the application to specific councils requires expertise on what was actually decided, ratified, and carried canonical force. Examples: Constance 1414–1418 (anti-conciliar vs conciliar resolution); Florence 1431–1449 (multiple sessions); Trent 1545–1563 (multi-decade run with disciplinary canons distinct from doctrinal output); contested ecumenical status (Pisa 1409, Constantinople IV).

The two reviewers may rule independently on Q1 and Q4 (per-jurisdiction split permitted, parallel to RT-2026-004 Q2 precedent). Q2, Q3, Q5 are Guardian-primary; the domain consultant is invited to flag any historiographic objection to a Guardian ruling on these but does not co-rule.

## 2. Questions

Five concrete questions. Each invites a binding ruling. "Defer" is acceptable only where the ruling depends on extraction-pass evidence the v0.1 protocol does not yet have.

### Q1. Inclusion-test boundary cases (Q-RE1 + new boundary audit) — domain-consultant primary, Guardian R4 cross-check

TC-07 v0.1 §4 explicitly excludes "pastoral instruction without canonical force" — encyclicals and motu-proprio actions that exhort but do not change canon law. The boundary between "exhortative" and "canonically operative" papal acts is contested; some twentieth-century papal acts have arguably done canonical work without conciliar form. Q-RE1 in TC-07 v0.1 §9 flags this for domain-consultant review.

**v0.2 expansion.** Beyond Q-RE1, three additional boundary cases require domain-consultant ruling:
- **Anti-pope councils (Pisa 1409; the Avignon-line councils during the Western Schism).** Currently outside the canonical 21 ecumenical councils; should TC-07 §3.3 multi-domain-legitimacy applicability admit them as inclusion candidates with explicit annotation, or hard-exclude?
- **Multi-session councils (Trent 1545–1563; Vatican II 1962–1965).** Should each session count as a distinct event under TC-07 §3.1 documentary-ratification, or only the closing ratification? The choice materially affects per-event spacing and therefore the n that flows to TC-06's three-tier power rule.
- **Conciliar resolutions vs papal ratifications.** Where a council's decisions were not papally ratified for years (or never), does the council date or the ratification date supply the §3.1 documentary-ratification timestamp?

**Ruling required.** Per-boundary explicit ruling: include / exclude / annotate. For multi-session councils, choose one of: (a) closing-ratification only, (b) per-session events with multi-tagging across structural / doctrinal / disciplinary sub-types per session, (c) hybrid (closing as primary, sessions as supplementary annotations). Guardian's R4 cross-check verifies the choice does not create a rescue-by-relabelling surface.

### Q2. Multi-tagging strictness × TC-06 v0.2 three-tier power rule cross-card coupling — Guardian primary

TC-07 §6 forbids predominance-based single-tagging: where evidence is positive for two or three sub-types, multi-tag. This was an explicit Guardian-veto target (Q-RE3 in v0.1).

RT-2026-002 Q2(b) installed a three-tier power rule in TC-06 v0.2: n<8 strict concordance + inconclusive default; 8≤n<15 weak concordance with mandatory MDE; n≥15 strict.

**The cross-card interaction.** Strict multi-tagging distributes events across sub-types. With ~21 ecumenical councils total and frequent multi-tagging, effective n per sub-type subset falls to ~8–14 — the weak-concordance band. This is exactly where Scout's Q4 of RT-2026-001 flagged the over-inconclusiveness risk. The interaction has three components Guardian must rule on:

- **Q2(a) Multi-tagging strictness — ratify, modify, or replace.** Three options:
  - (a) Ratify v0.1 §6 strict multi-tagging unchanged.
  - (b) Modify to allow predominance-based single-tagging with declared reason and Guardian re-authorisation, where doing so would push effective n above 15 for the dominant sub-type while still recording the secondary sub-type as supplementary annotation.
  - (c) Guardian-specified alternative.
- **Q2(b) Tag-set layer consistency.** TC-07's three sub-type tags (doctrinal / structural / disciplinary) are reform-event-type tags. TC-06 v0.2's six divergence-cause tags (`tenure-trend`, `succession-contested`, `coverage-gap`, `coverage-artifact`, `R8-residual-confirmed`, `none-identified`) operate on inter-event intervals. The two layers are distinct but interact at the per-event annotation: a multi-tagged event whose adjacent intervals carry `R8-residual-confirmed` divergence-cause tags is consequential for both R8 closure threshold (ii) and TC-07 sub-type stratification. Guardian rules: are the two tag layers operationally consistent in the v0.2 design, or does TC-07 v0.2 need a cross-layer consistency clause?
- **Q2(c) Effective-n reporting.** TC-07 v0.2 should require, for every sub-type-stratified analysis, explicit reporting of the effective n per sub-type subset, with cross-reference to TC-06 v0.2 §5 condition 4 tier assignment. Ratify, modify, or replace.

### Q3. Era-stratified inter-coder reliability sampling (SF-9 binding) — Guardian primary

SF-9 names multi-tagging era-heterogeneity inflation: modern councils (Trent, Vatican I, Vatican II) have richer documentary records than early-medieval ones, so strict multi-tagging produces higher multi-tag rates in recent eras, creating era-correlated sub-type stratification that looks like temporal trend but is source-heterogeneity bias.

TC-07 v0.1 §7 specifies inter-coder reliability testing on a sub-type-stratified calibration sample of 6–8 events. SF-9 binds v0.2 to *also* require era-stratified sampling.

**Ruling required.** Three options:

- (a) **Ratify SF-9 binding as drafted in horizon-signals.** TC-07 v0.2 §7 calibration sample becomes a 2-axis stratification (sub-type × era), with at least one event per era band per non-trivial sub-type combination. Cohort size grows from 6–8 to 12–18 events. Era bands match TC-06 v0.2 §4.3 boundaries (B1 325–1100 / B2 1100–1800 / B3 1800–1965).
- (b) **Modify** to era-stratification only (drop sub-type stratification on the calibration sample), with sub-type stratification verified post-pilot in the full extraction pass. Smaller calibration cohort, weaker discipline.
- (c) **Guardian-specified alternative,** including the option of size-by-era joint stratification with Guardian-pre-declared per-cell quotas.

Cohort-size growth is consequential for resource estimation; the calibration pilot must run *before* full extraction (TC-07 v0.1 §7.2 binding).

### Q4. Domain-consultant pass on inclusion/exclusion application — domain-consultant primary, Guardian R4 cross-check

The TC-07 §3 inclusion test and §4 exclusion test are operationally specified at the protocol level. The domain consultant is invited to apply the protocol to a calibration corpus of the 21 ecumenical councils and flag any inclusion/exclusion ruling that is operationally ambiguous, historiographically contested, or generates a rescue-by-relabelling surface.

**Specific consultant prompts:**

- **Lateran IV (1215).** Decretals Lateran IV produced (e.g., Cum ex iniuncto on annual confession, the heretic-prosecution canons) — multi-tagged across structural (papal authority over local episcopate) + doctrinal (transubstantiation defined) + disciplinary (annual confession requirement) per TC-07 §6, with each tag independently passing §3 inclusion. Confirm or contest.
- **Constance (1414–1418).** Conciliar-supremacy decree Haec sancta (1415) — does this pass §3.2 structural-change-in-decision-rights, given it was repudiated by subsequent papal action? Per-discipline split possible: R3 admits as defunct-equivalent reform-event; R4 hard-excludes on motte-and-bailey grounds.
- **Vatican II (1962–1965).** Lumen Gentium (collegiality) — structural; Sacrosanctum Concilium (liturgical reform) — disciplinary if it changed binding obligations on members; Dignitatis Humanae (religious freedom) — doctrinal. Multi-tagging consistent? Confirm or contest.

**Ruling required.** Per-prompt domain-consultant ruling (include / exclude / annotate); Guardian R4 cross-check on each. Disagreements between domain consultant and Guardian get logged for Council deliberation rather than auto-resolved.
