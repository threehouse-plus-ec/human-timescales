## Per-question rulings

### Q1 — Declaration-as-mitigation R3-disciplined?

**Ruling: option (b), with two named clauses.** Declaration alone is surfacing, not resolution — the same verdict Scout issued on TC-06 Q1, applied here to R3. Two clauses required for v0.2:

1. **Joint-concordance requirement.** No R3 finding may be issued from either case alone; only when Templar and Dissolution-cohort point in the same direction (e.g., both show shorter inter-event intervals than the survivor reference, OR both show comparable intervals) does the paired-case verdict license inference. This parallels RT-2026-002 Q4's TC-04+TC-08 joint structure: logical AND, not OR.
2. **Pre-declared endogenous-vs-external-attack interpretive frame.** Before extraction, TC-08 v0.2 must specify which observations would count as "endogenous reform-cycle finding" and which as "external-attack-vulnerability finding." Declaration after data examination is rescue-by-relabelling — exactly the R4 motte-and-bailey move RT-2026-002 Q3 disciplined for the channel-divergence-cause tags, transposed to R3.

### Q2 — Templar band-vs-archive conflict (per-discipline split ruled)

**Ruling: option (d) per-discipline split.** R3-discipline = (b); R8-closure-precondition = (c).

- **R3-discipline ruling: (b) pre-declared band-presumption override for Templar.** The Templar archive's papal-vs-internal asymmetry is itself the survivorship pattern R3 is meant to characterise (per memo §3.2 + condition 4). Defaulting Templar dominance to papal-ratification with internal as supplementary is methodologically appropriate for R3 purposes. Override logged in TC-08 v0.2 §3.2; the override does not propagate to other 1100–1800 cases under TC-06's Q2(a) band presumption.
- **R8-closure-precondition ruling: (c) hard exclusion.** Threshold (i)'s factor-of-2 channel-ratio bound cannot be satisfied by the Templar subset: the internal channel is documentary-survival-suppressed at a level that mechanical channel-ratio computation will register as far in excess of factor 2 from any cross-case median. The override mechanism in (b) renames the suppression as a declared-asymmetry; it does not arithmetically remove it from the channel ratio. Templar is therefore an R3 control without R8-closure standing.

**Consequence for TC-08's R8-closure contribution.** TC-08's R8 contribution rests on the Dissolution cohort alone. This makes Q4's threshold-(iii) ruling load-bearing: if the Dissolution cohort cannot itself satisfy threshold (iii), TC-08 fails to contribute to R8 closure at all and R8 closure pathway requires re-design.

### Q3 — Doctrinal absence-as-finding (single ruling)

**Ruling: option (c) pre-declared interpretive rule.** Doctrinal absence is reportable as finding only when accompanied by a positive structural-or-disciplinary-rate signal that is non-trivially different from a generic Poisson null *and* from the Catholic-councils baseline. Otherwise it is coverage gap.

This option is doubly disciplined. First, it gates the absence-as-finding reading on positive evidence in adjacent strata, removing the rescue-by-relabelling route. Second, it inhibits the `R8-residual-confirmed` tag (per refinement 2.1 of the brief's threshold-(ii) mapping) from going modal: tagging Templar's doctrinal-absence stratum as "examined and confirmed R8 residual" is contingent on positive signal in adjacent strata, not the default reading. Threshold (ii) is therefore proceduralised at the tag-application layer, not just the verdict-issuance layer.

### Q4 — Dissolution-cohort sampling (Guardian-specified alternative; cross-discipline complementary clauses)

**Ruling: Guardian-specified alternative built on option (a) with binding cross-discipline clauses.** Stratified-by-size design — 4 large / 4 medium / 4 small by ≥1535 income, pre-declared thresholds — with the following binding additions:

1. **Cohort minimum size = 12** (top of the memo's 8–12 range), not the bottom. Size-stratification needs ≥4 per cell to be operationally meaningful.
2. **Order distribution as reported covariate, not stratification axis.** Cohort must include representation from ≥3 distinct orders (Benedictine, Cluniac/Cistercian, Augustinian or Carthusian); actual distribution declared pre-extraction. Joint size-by-order stratification (option d) is rejected as infeasible at n = 12 (5 orders × 3 sizes = 15 cells > cohort).
3. **Threshold-(iii) anchor house — binding.** At least one of the four large houses must have ≥15 documentable pre-Dissolution reform episodes, identified pre-extraction. This house runs under TC-06 strict concordance per RT-2026-002 Q2(b), serving as the cohort's threshold-(iii) anchor. Without this anchor, the cohort runs entirely in the 8 ≤ n < 15 weak-concordance band per house and threshold (iii) fails. Candidate large houses: Glastonbury (~9-century lifespan; pre-Dissolution reform episodes including Dunstan 10c, post-Norman, 1184 fire and rebuild, Black Death, late-medieval) is the most plausible n≥15 candidate; Bury St Edmunds and Westminster are secondaries.

**Per-discipline mapping:** R3-discipline accepts clauses 1–2 (size-stratification with order covariate). R8-closure-precondition adds clause 3 (anchor house). The clauses are complementary, not divergent — Author drafts a single Q4 protocol satisfying all three. If no plausible n ≥ 15 anchor house exists in the candidate field at v0.2 drafting, Author flags as blocker and Guardian re-deliberates Q4 specifically; do not proceed past v0.2 §4.2 without resolution.

### Q5 — R3 closure call + joint-structure ruling

**Status ruling: option (b) procedurally-managed-but-live**, parallel to R8.

**Joint-structure ruling: option (β) inherit structure with R3-tuned thresholds.** R3 closure requires TC-04 + TC-08 joint completion (TC-04 supplies the survivor-side cross-institution reference distribution; without it, R3 has no survivor reference at H₀-relevant generality), with three R3-tuned thresholds:

- **R3 threshold (i): survivor-vs-defunct rate-ratio bound.** The per-stratum reform-event rate (events per century, sub-type-specific) for the survivor reference (TC-01 + TC-04 cohort) versus the defunct reference (TC-08 paired-case set) varies by no more than a factor of 2. Mirrors R8's factor-of-2 structure for symmetry; the substantive content differs (rate-ratio not channel-ratio).
- **R3 threshold (ii): no rescue-by-relabelling modal pattern.** Neither "external-attack-vulnerability" nor "endogenous-reform-cycle" reading is the modal interpretation across the TC-08 paired-case set; if either is modal, R3's pre-declared interpretive frame (Q1 clause 2) has been silently relaxed and R3 stays live.
- **R3 threshold (iii): shared with R8.** Cross-form coverage (TC-04 + TC-08) without weak-concordance modal regime, identical to R8's threshold (iii). The disciplines share this threshold because both are exposed to the same small-n verdict-issuance vulnerability.

On satisfaction of all three R3 thresholds plus joint TC-04+TC-08 completion, R3 status moves to **closed-conditional-on-thresholds**, with thresholds subject to Guardian re-authorisation if any TC-04 or TC-08 finding exposes their fragility. Until then, every survivor-stationarity claim issued under TC-01 carries a live R3 residual that must be declared in the claim's annotation, parallel to the R8 residual prescribed by RT-2026-001's closure call.

Option (α) inherit-identical is rejected: R8 channel-ratio and R3 rate-ratio are not interchangeable measurements. Option (γ) diverge is rejected: R3 cannot license a survivor-vs-defunct comparison without a TC-04-backed survivor-side reference at H₀-relevant generality (Catholic councils alone is one institution; the comparison needs cross-institution anchoring on the survivor side).

## Net-new R3-discipline risks

**SF-11 — Meta-survivorship in candidate-set selection.** The candidate-set of "defunct institutions documented well enough for M1 work" is itself a survivorship filter (memo §2 condition 3 dating-precision and condition 4 survival-asymmetry-assessable both operate as pre-filters). Two distinct mechanisms operate:

- **Documentary-survival pre-filter.** Dissolutions whose records did not survive the dissolution circumstance are absent from the candidate set entirely. Templars survived because the Vatican kept the bulls and Curzon recovered the statutes; Knights of Calatrava (dissolved 1808–1873) have less surviving internal record and are not on the candidate list. The candidate field is documentary-survivor-biased.
- **Historiographic-fashion pre-filter.** Templars are heavily studied; obscure dissolved orders are not. The historiographic literature is the analyst's pre-extraction filter, and it has its own asymmetries (papal-suppression cases over-represented; quiet-attrition cases under-represented).

**Verdict: lodge as SF-11.** Status: PROCEDURALLY-MITIGATABLE-IN-V0.2, LIVE pending v0.2 mitigation. Mitigation: TC-08 v0.2 §6 must declare in writing the specific dissolutions considered and rejected, the rejection criteria, and a brief sensitivity statement on whether the candidate field's documentary-survival and historiographic-fashion biases plausibly shift the paired-case verdict.

No promotion to risk register (R11+); SF-11 is procedurally-mitigatable at the v0.2 layer. If v0.2's declared candidate field fails to address the asymmetries credibly, Guardian reserves right to promote at the next review pass.
