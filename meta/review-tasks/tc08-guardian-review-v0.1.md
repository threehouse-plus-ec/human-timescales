## 1. Why Guardian, why this card, why now

R3 (survivorship bias) is Scout-originated (Scout signal 2 in v0.1 scoping review) and Guardian-disciplined. Scout flagged the horizon risk: TC-01 draws from institutions that survived; if failed institutions had systematically shorter reform cycles, observed survivor-stationarity is a selection effect, not a finding about underlying L₅ rates. TC-08 attempts the structural answer — feasibility check on at least one defunct archive at the resolution M1 requires.

The TC-08 task card names Guardian (R3-discipline) as reviewer. The v0.1 memo (drafted Author 2026-04-27) issues a paired-case feasibility verdict: Knights Templar (1119–1312) primary + English Dissolution cohort (1536–1541, 8–12 monastic houses) secondary. Each adopted case is qualified with declared caveats; two candidate cases (Society of Jesus, failed corporations) are declined with reasoning.

Guardian's primary jurisdiction here is R3-discipline: rule on whether the paired-case feasibility verdict **actually disciplines survivorship bias**, or whether it nominally closes R3 while leaving the underlying horizon risk live behind a "declared in writing" mitigation. The cross-brief parallel still bites: Scout's Q1 on RT-2026-001 asked "does dual measurement resolve R8 or merely surface it?" — the same structural question recurs here for R3 under "declaration as mitigation."

**Cross-brief escalation per RT-2026-002 Q4 binding ruling.** RT-2026-002 closed 2026-04-28 with Guardian's Q4 ruling broadening R8 closure to require *both* TC-04 *and* TC-08, plus three pre-declared operational thresholds: **(i) per-interval channel-ratio bound** (variation by no more than a factor of 2 from the cross-case median across all paired intervals in TC-04 and TC-08); **(ii) no tag-cluster dominance** (neither `tenure-trend` nor `R8-residual-confirmed` modal in any TC-04 case or in the TC-08 paired-case set); **(iii) cross-form coverage without weak-concordance modal regime** (at least one TC-04 case and at least one TC-08 case characterised without invoking the RT-2026-002 Q2(b) lower-tier weak-concordance regime as the modal verdict-issuance pathway). TC-08 is therefore no longer only an R3 control deliverable; it is now an operational predicate for R8 closure.

Guardian's review pass must accordingly cover **two disciplines in this single brief**: R3 (primary) and R8-closure-precondition adequacy (cross-binding from RT-2026-002). The two disciplines are not identical and may produce different rulings on the same TC-08 design choice — e.g., R3 might tolerate a Templar-internal documentary correction that R8-closure-precondition disallows because the correction relies on weak-concordance reasoning. Where the disciplines diverge, Guardian is invited to issue separate rulings per discipline; the response memo's structure should make the per-discipline split explicit when it occurs.

**Where the three RT-2026-002 Q4 thresholds bite in this brief.** Threshold (i) channel-ratio bound bites **Q2** (Templar asymmetric documentary survival; the band-vs-archive conflict described there) and **Q4** (Dissolution-cohort sampling, where institution-internal-time clocks are heterogeneous across Benedictine / Cluniac / Cistercian / Augustinian / Carthusian successor lines). Threshold (ii) tag-cluster non-dominance bites **Q3** (doctrinal absence-as-finding, which interacts with the expanded six-tag set Guardian's RT-2026-002 Q3 ratified — `tenure-trend`, `succession-contested`, `coverage-gap`, `coverage-artifact`, `R8-residual-confirmed`, `none-identified`) and the **§3 catch-all** (where the meta-survivorship risk may merit its own tag). Threshold (iii) cross-form coverage without weak-concordance modal bites **Q4** (cohort sampling discipline) and the small-n power floor that RT-2026-002 Q2(b)'s tier rule imposes on TC-08's strata (Templar n ≈ 7–8 reform events; Dissolution cohort n ≈ 8–12 houses) — the entire TC-08 paired-case set sits in Q2(b)'s 8 ≤ n < 15 weak-concordance-permitted band, so threshold (iii) requires Guardian to rule on whether TC-08 may rely on weak concordance at all without disqualifying itself as an R8-closure component.

The brief's question structure (Q1–Q5) and out-of-scope declarations (§5) are unchanged; the substantive shift is that Guardian's rulings now feed two outputs each (R3 disposition + R8-closure-precondition disposition) where the disciplines bear on the same design choice.

## 2. Questions

Five concrete questions. Each invites a binding ruling. "Defer" is acceptable only where the ruling depends on data the memo does not yet have.

### Q1. Is "condition-5 partial failure, with declaration in writing" R3-disciplined enough?

Both adopted cases fail condition 5 (dissolution generality) partially: Templar suppression is over-determined by Philip IV's debt and Avignonese constraints; the English Dissolution is over-determined by Henry VIII's break with Rome and Cromwell's administrative project. The memo (§3.3, §4.2) acknowledges both as partial failures and proposes "explicit declaration in the eventual TC-09 ledger entry" as the mitigation.

R3-discipline question: does written declaration actually correct the survivorship-bias inference, or does it merely render the limitation visible while leaving the inference uncorrected? This is the R3 analogue of Scout's Q1 on TC-06 (surfacing ≠ resolving). If declaration is sufficient, the v0.1 mitigation stands; if not, an additional protocol clause is required before the paired-case verdict can license an R3 finding.

**Ruling required.** Three options:

- (a) **Ratify "declaration as mitigation"** as procedurally sufficient. TC-09 carries the declaration; downstream readers infer accordingly.
- (b) **Require additional discipline.** Examples: hard exclusion of any R3-closure claim drawn from either case alone (only joint concordance licenses inference); explicit pre-declared interpretive frame distinguishing "endogenous reform-cycle finding" from "external-attack-vulnerability finding"; weighting scheme that down-weights over-determined dissolutions.
- (c) **Guardian-specified alternative.**

### Q2. Templar asymmetric documentary survival — is annotation sufficient, or is structural correction required?

Memo §3.2 documents severe within-Templar asymmetry: Vatican-side bulls and council acta survive at high rate; internal Templar chapter records and statute corpus survive only patchily through Curzon (1886) and a few later editions. Mitigation proposed: "explicit declaration of papal-versus-internal documentary asymmetry."

R3-discipline question, with cross-brief implications: under TC-06's channel-concordance rule (§5 condition 4), systematic under-counting of internally-ratified events biases the channel ratio toward papal-ratification — exactly the kind of unexamined temporal-parallax R8 was meant to catch. The R3 question and the R8 question converge here.

**Band-vs-archive conflict from RT-2026-002 Q2(a) landing.** RT-2026-002 Q2(a) has landed as ratify-with-modification: **presumptive channel-dominance by era band**, with institution-internal-time presumptive-authoritative for the 1100–1800 band that fully covers the Templar lifespan (1119–1312). The Templar documentary record (per memo §3.2) is dominantly papal-side — exactly inverting the band default. This is a conflict the band rule alone cannot resolve: applying the band presumption mechanically would require treating the patchily-surviving internal Templar record as authoritative over the well-surviving papal record, which is operationally absurd. Guardian must rule on which side gives way for the Templar subset and pre-declare the override mechanism. The R8-closure-precondition discipline (threshold (i), channel-ratio bound) bites here too: if the per-interval channel ratio for Templar systematically exceeds the factor-of-2 bound because the internal channel is documentary-survival-suppressed, the Templar subset alone cannot satisfy R8 closure threshold (i) regardless of how the band override is declared.

**Ruling required.** Four options (R3-discipline ruling and R8-closure-precondition ruling may diverge):

- (a) **Ratify "annotation only,"** with the asymmetry documented but no quantitative correction applied. The RT-2026-002 Q2(a) band presumption stands; analyst notes the asymmetry per-interval; no override is invoked.
- (b) **Pre-declared band-presumption override for Templar.** Override the RT-2026-002 Q2(a) institution-internal-presumptive default for the Templar subset specifically; default Templar dominance to papal-ratification channel; report internal-channel results as supplementary, not concordance-eligible. Override logged in TC-08 v0.2 §3.2 and ratified by Guardian re-authorisation; the override does not propagate to other 1100–1800 cases.
- (c) **Hard exclusion.** Templar subset cannot generate channel-concordance stationarity verdicts under TC-06 §5 condition 4; Templar data feeds rate-comparison and pattern-comparison only, not stationarity-claim machinery. R8-closure-precondition implication: TC-08's R8 contribution comes through the Dissolution cohort alone, with the Templar treated as R3 control without R8-closure standing.
- (d) **Guardian-specified alternative,** including the option of accepting (a) for R3-discipline purposes while ruling that (c) applies for R8-closure-precondition purposes — a declared per-discipline split.

### Q3. Doctrinal sub-type absence — finding, or coverage gap? (R3 ↔ R4 motte-and-bailey audit)

Memo §3.3 and §6.2 hold that the Templar archive's absence of doctrinal-sub-type reform events is "itself a finding, not a coverage gap." This is methodologically aggressive: it converts a TC-07-stratification structural incompatibility (the Templar archive cannot deliver one of the three TC-07 sub-types) into substantive content.

R3-discipline question, with R4 motte-and-bailey concern: a defunct military order whose archive yields zero doctrinal events may not be cross-comparable with the Catholic-councils archive that yields many — the "absence-as-finding" move risks rescue-by-relabelling (parallel to RT-2026-002 Q3's audit of TC-06 §4.4 divergence-cause tags). Under empirical pressure, "we found doctrinal absence" can morph between two readings: (i) a positive substantive finding about military-order reform dynamics; (ii) a coverage-gap admission redrawn as content. The two readings have different licence to feed TC-09 inference.

**Ruling required.** Three options:

- (a) **Ratify "absence-as-finding"** as drafted, with the proviso that the eventual TC-09 entry distinguishes the two readings explicitly.
- (b) **Reclassify as coverage gap.** Restrict TC-08 → TC-09 inferences to structural and disciplinary sub-types only, with explicit non-comparability declaration on the doctrinal stratum.
- (c) **Pre-declared interpretive rule.** Doctrinal absence is reportable as finding *only when accompanied* by a positive structural-or-disciplinary-rate signal that is itself non-trivially different from a generic Poisson null and from the Catholic-councils baseline; otherwise absence is coverage gap.

### Q4. Dissolution-cohort sampling — what discipline binds the cohort selection protocol?

Memo §4.2 proposes 8–12 monastic houses sampled with mixed inclusion (not larger-houses-only) and "explicit per-house survival-profile annotation." Q-DA2 in §7 flags the open question: larger-houses-only (cleaner data) versus mixed (the survival asymmetry is itself the target of characterisation).

R3-discipline question: cohort-level analysis is itself vulnerable to within-cohort survivorship bias — documentary survival correlates with size, wealth, and order affiliation. The drafter's "mixed, with annotation" defers the discipline to extraction-time annotation; Guardian may rule that the discipline must be in the *sample selection*, pre-declared, not in post-hoc per-house annotation.

**Ruling required.** Four options:

- (a) **Stratified-by-size design.** Pre-declared thresholds (e.g., 4 large / 4 medium / 4 small by ≥1535 income); within-stratum random or quota selection.
- (b) **Stratified-by-order design.** Pre-declared distribution across Benedictine / Cluniac / Cistercian / Augustinian / Carthusian; within-order selection by other criteria.
- (c) **Drafter's "mixed with annotation"** as proposed.
- (d) **Guardian-specified alternative,** including the option of requiring both Q4(a) and Q4(b) (size-by-order joint stratification) if cohort size permits.

The ruling binds the sample-selection protocol; deferring to TC-09 analyst-time re-imports the within-cohort survivorship bias the cohort approach was meant to mitigate.

### Q5. R3 closure call — Guardian's R3-discipline ruling

Parallel to Scout's R8 closure call on TC-06 (RT-2026-001 §3 closure paragraph). Guardian to rule on R3 status after TC-08 v0.1 plus the anticipated v0.2 (incorporating this brief's response and proceeding to extraction):

- (a) **Closed.** Survivorship bias is procedurally controlled by the paired-case verdict; R3 leaves the active register.
- (b) **Procedurally-managed-but-live.** Paired cases mitigate but do not resolve; R3 stays as horizon risk under named procedural mitigation. Guardian to specify the closure condition that would move R3 to (a).
- (c) **Live.** Paired-case approach is insufficient; R3 requires a third defunct case, a different methodological strategy, or both.

Guardian is also invited to rule on the joint-closure-structure question: RT-2026-002 Q4 has now crystallised a TC-04+TC-08 joint-closure structure for **R8** (with three Guardian-pre-declared thresholds). Does **R3** closure inherit the same structure, or does it differ?

- **(α) Inherit identical structure.** R3 closure also requires TC-04 + TC-08 joint completion plus the same three thresholds. The two disciplines collapse onto a single closure pathway.
- **(β) Inherit structure with R3-specific thresholds.** R3 closure requires TC-04 + TC-08 joint completion, but with thresholds tuned to survivorship-bias discipline rather than temporal-parallax discipline (e.g., a survivor-vs-defunct rate-ratio bound replacing R8's channel-ratio bound).
- **(γ) Diverge.** R3 closure proceeds independently of R8 closure on a TC-08-only pathway; R8 closure's TC-04 dependency does not apply to R3 because TC-04 is a survivor-side cross-institution check that does not bear on the survivor-vs-defunct comparison.

Guardian's ruling here is load-bearing for v0.3 of the scoping document's risk register and for the eventual TC-09 ledger entry's R3 annotation.
