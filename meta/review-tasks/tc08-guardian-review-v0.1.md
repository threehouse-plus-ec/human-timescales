## 2. Questions

Five concrete questions. Each invites a binding ruling. "Defer" is acceptable only where the ruling depends on data the memo does not yet have.

### Q1. Is "condition-5 partial failure, with declaration in writing" R3-disciplined enough?

Both adopted cases fail condition 5 (dissolution generality) partially: Templar suppression is over-determined by Philip IV's debt and Avignonese constraints; the English Dissolution is over-determined by Henry VIII's break with Rome and Cromwell's administrative project. The memo (§3.3, §4.2) acknowledges both as partial failures and proposes "explicit declaration in the eventual TC-09 ledger entry" as the mitigation.

R3-discipline question: does written declaration actually correct the survivorship-bias inference, or does it merely render the limitation visible while leaving the inference uncorrected? This is the R3 analogue of Scout's Q1 on TC-06 (surfacing ≠ resolving). If declaration is sufficient, the v0.1 mitigation stands; if not, an additional protocol clause is required before the paired-case verdict can license an R3 finding.

**Ruling required.** Three options:

- (a) **Ratify "declaration as mitigation"** as procedurally sufficient. TC-09 carries the declaration; downstream readers infer accordingly.
- (b) **Require additional discipline.** Examples: hard exclusion of any R3-closure claim drawn from either case alone (only joint concordance licenses inference); explicit pre-declared interpretive frame distinguishing "endogenous reform-cycle finding" from "external-attack-vulnerability finding"; weighting scheme that down-weights over-determined dissolutions.
- (c) **Guardian-specified alternative.**

### Q2. Templar asymmetric documentary survival — is annotation sufficient, or is structural correction required?

Memo §3.2 documents severe within-Templar asymmetry: Vatican-side bulls and council acta survive at high rate; internal Templar chapter records and statute corpus survive only patchily through Curzon (1886) and a few later editions. Mitigation proposed: "explicit declaration of papal-versus-internal documentary asymmetry."

R3-discipline question, with cross-brief implications: under TC-06's channel-concordance rule (§5 condition 4), systematic under-counting of internally-ratified events biases the channel ratio toward papal-ratification — exactly the kind of unexamined temporal-parallax R8 was meant to catch. The R3 question and the R8 question converge here. Guardian's RT-2026-002 Q2(a) ruling on channel-dominance annotation is directly load-bearing: if Q2(a) requires per-interval channel-dominance annotation, that discipline imports cleanly to TC-08's Templar subset; if Q2(a) is rejected, TC-08 needs its own correction.

**Ruling required.** Three options:

- (a) **Ratify "annotation only,"** with the asymmetry documented but no quantitative correction applied. Suitable if RT-2026-002 Q2(a) lands as ratify.
- (b) **Require coverage-gap correction or channel-dominance presumption.** Default Templar dominance assignment to papal-ratification channel; report internal-channel results as supplementary, not concordance-eligible.
- (c) **Hard exclusion.** Templar subset cannot generate channel-concordance stationarity verdicts under TC-06 §5 condition 4; Templar data feeds rate-comparison and pattern-comparison only, not stationarity-claim machinery.

### Q3. Doctrinal sub-type absence — finding, or coverage gap? (R3 ↔ R4 motte-and-bailey audit)

Memo §3.3 and §6.2 hold that the Templar archive's absence of doctrinal-sub-type reform events is "itself a finding, not a coverage gap." This is methodologically aggressive: it converts a TC-07-stratification structural incompatibility (the Templar archive cannot deliver one of the three TC-07 sub-types) into substantive content.

R3-discipline question, with R4 motte-and-bailey concern: a defunct military order whose archive yields zero doctrinal events may not be cross-comparable with the Catholic-councils archive that yields many — the "absence-as-finding" move risks rescue-by-relabelling (parallel to RT-2026-002 Q3's audit of TC-06 §4.4 divergence-cause tags). Under empirical pressure, "we found doctrinal absence" can morph between two readings: (i) a positive substantive finding about military-order reform dynamics; (ii) a coverage-gap admission redrawn as content. The two readings have different licence to feed TC-09 inference.

**Ruling required.** Three options:

- (a) **Ratify "absence-as-finding"** as drafted, with the proviso that the eventual TC-09 entry distinguishes the two readings explicitly.
- (b) **Reclassify as coverage gap.** Restrict TC-08 → TC-09 inferences to structural and disciplinary sub-types only, with explicit non-comparability declaration on the doctrinal stratum.
- (c) **Pre-declared interpretive rule.** Doctrinal absence is reportable as finding *only when accompanied* by a positive structural-or-disciplinary-rate signal that is itself non-trivially different from a generic Poisson null and from the Catholic-councils baseline; otherwise absence is coverage gap.

### Q4. Dissolution-cohort sampling — what discipline binds the cohort selection protocol?

Memo §4.2 proposes 8–12 monastic houses sampled with mixed inclusion (not larger-houses-only) and "explicit per-house survival-profile annotation." Q-DA2 in §7 flags the open question: larger-houses-only (cleaner data) versus mixed (the survival asymmetry is itself the target of characterisation).

R3-discipline question: cohort-level analysis is itself vulnerable to within-cohort survivorship bias — documentary survival correlates with size, wealth, and order affiliation. The drafter's "mixed, with annotation" defers the discipline to extraction-time annotation; Guardian may rule that the discipline must be in the *sample selection*, pre-declared, not in post-hoc per-house annotation.

**Ruling required.** Four options:

- (a) **Stratified-by-size design.** Pre-declared thresholds (e.g., 4 large / 4 medium / 4 small by ≥1535 income); within-stratum random or quota selection.
- (b) **Stratified-by-order design.** Pre-declared distribution across Benedictine / Cluniac / Cistercian / Augustinian / Carthusian; within-order selection by other criteria.
- (c) **Drafter's "mixed with annotation"** as proposed.
- (d) **Guardian-specified alternative,** including the option of requiring both Q4(a) and Q4(b) (size-by-order joint stratification) if cohort size permits.

The ruling binds the sample-selection protocol; deferring to TC-09 analyst-time re-imports the within-cohort survivorship bias the cohort approach was meant to mitigate.
