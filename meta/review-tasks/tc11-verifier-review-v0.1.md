TC-11's drafter explicitly acknowledged that bibliography assembly is a known LLM-failure mode (citation fabrication). ### Q2. Page-level confirmation for key factual claims

The memo makes specific factual claims attributed to sources. Where v0.3 §12 will quote or summarise these claims, page-level references are required. Verifier's task: confirm each claim against the cited source at page-or-section precision.

Specific claims worth confirming (representative, not exhaustive):

- **Mannheim 1928** — "distinguishes biological generation, generational location, and generational unit." Confirm the three-fold distinction at section level.
- **Stinchcombe 1965** — "imprinting finding: founding-era social conditions persist in organisations across decades through their embedded role structures." Confirm at section level (commonly cited as Stinchcombe's "Liability of Newness" section, but the imprinting argument is distinct and runs in a different part of the chapter).
- **Olson 1982** — "long-stable polities accumulate distributional coalitions." Confirm at chapter level.
- **North 1990** — "informal norms ... change far more slowly than the formal rules themselves." Confirm at chapter level (commonly Chapter 4 or 5).
- **Hannan & Freeman 1977** — "structural inertia thesis." Confirm at section level.
- **Baron & Hannan 2002** — "five HRM systems"; "three times more likely to fail if the founder's blueprint is altered." Confirm at section level (the five-systems typology is the central exhibit).
- **Henderson, Miller, Hambrick 2006** — "in dynamic industries, performance peaks early and decays." Confirm the headline empirical finding at section/figure level.
- **Kaplan & Minton 2012** — "average CEO tenure under 7 years over 1992–2007 sample"; "total turnover rate 15.8% per annum." Confirm at table level.
- **Spencer Stuart 2025** — five practitioner-data claims: 168 new CEOs in 2025; 84% first-time enterprise CEOs; average incoming-CEO age 54.4; average CEO tenure 8.5 years (down from 9.1 in 2021). Confirm against the report's methodology section, with explicit note on whether the figures survive industry-mix and pandemic controls (this also serves Q5 below).
- **Azoulay et al. 2019** — "non-collaborators … draw upon a different scientific corpus and are disproportionately likely to be highly cited." Confirm at section level.

**Deliverable.** A page-level confirmation table: claim → source → page/section → confirmed / discrepancy-flagged. Discrepancies trigger annotation revision in TC-11 v0.2.

### Q3. Coverage-gap closure — four named gaps

The memo §4 names four gaps the Verifier should close before v0.3 §12 incorporation. For each, Verifier adds annotated citations meeting the per-band per-criterion guidance below.

**(a) Post-2015 contemporary empirical work on tenure variance, cohort effects, executive turnover.** Target: 2–3 sources, peer-reviewed, post-2015. Should specifically address H₁ second-order signatures (variance growth, kurtosis, reversal frequency) — *not* mean-tenure-only studies, which are already covered by [11] and [12].

**(b) Religious-order succession.** Target: 1–2 sources on contemporary academic literature on religious-order generational succession. Comparative scope welcome (Buddhist sangha succession; Sufi tariqa lineages; Eastern Orthodox monastic succession) given TC-01 sits on Catholic councils and additional comparison breadth strengthens the M3 diversification argument.

**(c) AI-mediated coordination disruption.** Target: 2 post-2023 sources on AI-mediated authority, AI-augmented decision-making in institutional settings, or LLM-mediated consensus formation. This band is load-bearing for FH-005 (M-Conjecture expiry-clock). Sources may be working papers / preprints if peer-reviewed coverage is thin, with explicit flag.

**(d) Comparative-international literature.** Target: 1–2 sources on European institutional tenure, comparative parliamentary work, or comparative-historical work on long-lived non-Western institutions (Chinese imperial bureaucracy, Ottoman ulama, Japanese shogunate succession). Resolves Q-BF3.

**Deliverable.** 6–9 new annotated entries lodged in the response memo's §3, formatted parallel to the existing memo's §3 entries (full citation; "Bears on" tag; annotation linking to H₀ / H₁ / M-Conjecture; Verifier note on methodological reliability).

### Q5. Selection-bias adequacy and Spencer Stuart [12] methodological scrutiny

- **(a) Selection-bias check.** The TC-11 card flags selection bias as the primary risk. The memo §2 declares a search strategy. Verifier confirms the strategy is sufficient, or proposes specific tightenings (e.g., requiring inverse-citation searches; or requiring a non-English-language search round; or requiring a hostile-to-thesis source-band search to detect confirmation bias).

If they do not, the memo's framing of [12] as evidence for H₁ second-order signatures must be qualified or retracted. **Deliverable.** Selection-bias verdict (sufficient / propose tightening with named tightening); Spencer Stuart [12] methodological verdict (qualifies / does not qualify as second-order H₁ evidence after controls).
